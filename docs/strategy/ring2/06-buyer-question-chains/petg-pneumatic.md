# Buyer Question Chain: PETG Pneumatic Tubes

## Personas Covered

- **PETG System Integrator (Dr. Chen)** — Hospital logistics system integrator, technical director or supply chain manager at a pneumatic tube system installation firm. Buys PETG tubes for hospital/lab pneumatic delivery systems. Non-negotiable requirements: zero-leak seal integrity, optical transparency for blockage detection, custom specs per project, and batch-to-batch consistency.

---

## Question Chain

### Stage 1: Awareness

> Trigger: New hospital project won, or existing supplier's quality has dropped. Dr. Chen is scanning for potential PETG tube suppliers who actually understand pneumatic delivery systems (not generic plastic tube sellers).

**Q1: "Does this manufacturer specialize in PETG pneumatic tubes, or is it just another general plastic pipe factory?"**

- Content needed: Dedicated PETG pneumatic tube section that speaks the integrator's language — pneumatic delivery systems, hospital logistics, lab automation. Must immediately signal "we understand your application" rather than listing generic tube specs.
- Key proof point: Show that Tianze supplies PETG tubes specifically for pneumatic systems, not as a sideline product.
- Positioning hook: "We make the machines that make the pipes" — applied to PETG context, this means in-house extrusion control = tighter dimensional tolerances.

**Q2: "What transparency level can they achieve? Can I actually see a blockage through the tube wall?"**

- Content needed: Transparency specifications with visual evidence — light transmittance percentage, photos/video of tubes showing visible contents, comparison against opaque alternatives. Hospital clients expect visual quality; integrators need functional transparency for blockage detection.
- Key proof point: Quantified transparency rating (e.g., light transmittance %) plus real-world visual demonstration.

**Q3: "What's their burst pressure rating? Will these tubes survive continuous pneumatic system operating pressure?"**

- Content needed: Pressure test data — burst pressure, continuous working pressure rating (integrators typically operate at 0.6-0.8 MPa), test methodology description. This is the first technical filter: if burst pressure is inadequate, no further conversation.
- Key proof point: Test report data showing burst pressure well above the 0.8 MPa continuous operating threshold.

---

### Stage 2: Comparison

> Dr. Chen has shortlisted 2-3 suppliers. Now requesting samples, running pressure tests, checking dimensional consistency. At this stage, the comparison is technical and data-driven — not about brand story.

**Q4: "What custom diameters and wall thicknesses can you produce? Every hospital layout is different."**

- Content needed: Full range of available OD (outer diameter), wall thickness, and cut-to-length options. Must show both standard range and custom capability. Dr. Chen's real question: "If I need OD 160mm, wall 3mm, cut to 6m — can you do it, or will you try to push me to your standard sizes?"
- Key proof point: Table of standard sizes + explicit statement of custom range (min/max OD, wall thickness, length) with MOQ for custom orders.

**Q5: "How consistent are your dimensions across batches? Our current supplier has quality fluctuations."**

- Content needed: Dimensional tolerance data (OD tolerance, wall thickness tolerance, ovality) plus QC process description. This is often the real reason integrators switch suppliers — not price, but inconsistency. If tube OD varies by 0.5mm between batches, fittings leak.
- Key proof point: Tolerance specs (e.g., OD +/-0.1mm), batch-to-batch consistency data, QC inspection protocol description. Connection to equipment narrative: in-house machine manufacturing = control over extrusion precision.

**Q6: "What seal test results can you show me? Zero leaks is non-negotiable — patient samples travel through these tubes."**

- Content needed: Seal integrity test methodology and results — air tightness test data, connection point leak test under operating pressure, accelerated aging test results if available. For pneumatic tube systems carrying lab specimens, a leak means contamination risk and hospital liability.
- Key proof point: Specific test data (not just "we test every batch" — show the method, the pass criteria, the rejection rate).

---

### Stage 3: Decision

> Dr. Chen has tested samples, confirmed technical fit. Now the questions shift to supply reliability, scale, and risk mitigation — because a multi-phase hospital project means 12-24 months of ongoing supply dependency.

**Q7: "Can you supply 2,000+ meters per month consistently for a multi-phase hospital project?"**

- Content needed: Production capacity information — monthly output capacity, lead time for standard vs. custom orders, ability to scale for large projects. Dr. Chen is committing to a supplier for a project that may span multiple hospital buildings across 1-2 years.
- Key proof point: Production capacity figures, on-time delivery track record, ability to maintain quality at volume.

**Q8: "Who else are you supplying? Can I speak to other integrators using your tubes?"**

- Content needed: Reference projects (anonymized if necessary) — project scale, application type (hospital pneumatic system, lab automation), supply duration. Dr. Chen trusts peer validation more than marketing claims.
- Key proof point: Anonymized case references ("Supplied 15,000m of custom OD 110mm PETG for a 500-bed hospital pneumatic system, 2024-2025") or willingness to connect with existing clients (with their consent).
- Positioning hook: Brand positioning notes "stable supply to major domestic integrators (reference without naming)" — this is the content to surface.

**Q9: "What happens if a batch fails QC? What's your defect handling and replacement protocol?"**

- Content needed: Quality assurance process — incoming material inspection, in-process checks, final inspection, defect rate statistics, and critically: the replacement/rework policy when issues are found. Integrators installing in hospital environments cannot afford project delays from defective materials.
- Key proof point: Documented QC workflow, stated defect handling SLA (replacement lead time), and evidence of proactive quality management (not just reactive rejection).

## Content Gaps

### Critical Gaps (blocks conversion at Decision stage)

1. **Dedicated PETG pneumatic product section** — Currently no page or section speaks directly to pneumatic tube system integrators. PETG is buried in the general product catalog. A technical buyer landing on the site cannot quickly confirm "this manufacturer understands my application."

2. **Technical test data package** — Burst pressure, seal integrity, dimensional tolerance, transparency rating. This is the core comparison material. Without it, Dr. Chen cannot shortlist Tianze — the sample request never happens.

3. **Reference projects / case studies** — Even anonymized references ("500-bed hospital, 15,000m supplied over 12 months") provide the peer validation that closes deals in this niche. Current site has zero.

### Important Gaps (weakens positioning but doesn't block)

4. **QC process documentation** — Integrators want to see the inspection workflow, not just "ISO 9001 certified." Show the steps: incoming material check, extrusion monitoring, dimensional sampling, pressure test, packaging inspection.

5. **Custom specification capability page** — The OEM page focuses on PVC molds. PETG custom tubes (non-standard OD, wall thickness, cut length) need their own treatment, or at minimum a clear section within the PETG product area.

6. **Supply stability proof** — Monthly capacity, lead time commitments, inventory policy for repeat clients. The "we make the machines" narrative is powerful here: in-house equipment = less dependency on external machine availability = more predictable output.

### Positioning Opportunity

7. **"Equipment manufacturer" narrative applied to PETG** — The brand's core differentiator ("we make the machines that make the pipes") is currently framed around PVC bending machines. For PETG, the equivalent narrative is extrusion control: "We control the extrusion process end-to-end, which is why our dimensional consistency beats suppliers who buy generic extrusion lines." This connection is not yet made anywhere on the site.
