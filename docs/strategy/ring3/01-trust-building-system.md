## The Trust Problem

Tianze's ideal buyers (distributors, contractors, manufacturers) are evaluating a Chinese factory they've never visited. The #1 barrier is trust, not product quality.

Competitors solve this with certification logos (Ledes: "China's first CSA-certified"). Tianze has fewer certifications but a stronger underlying story (equipment manufacturer). The trust system must compensate for certification gaps while leveraging the unique manufacturing narrative.

## Trust Narrative Arc

A visitor's trust builds progressively across pages:

```
Homepage (3 seconds):
  "This is a real factory that makes machines AND pipes" → initial credibility
    ↓
Product page (30 seconds):
  "Their specs meet my standard, here's the data" → technical confidence
    ↓
About page (60 seconds):
  "60+ people, 100 mu factory, 20+ countries, ISO certified" → scale confidence
    ↓
Free sample CTA:
  "They're confident enough to send samples for free" → commitment signal
    ↓
Inquiry form:
  Trust threshold crossed → conversion
```

## Certification Gap Compensation Score

How well does Tianze's trust system compensate for missing certifications?

| Buyer concern | Cert answer (if available) | Tianze's alternative | Compensation strength |
|--------------|--------------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| "Is this product safe to use?" | UL/CSA mark | Standards comparison table + test data + free sample | Strong (verifiable) |
| "Will this pass building inspection?" | UL listing number | "Engineered to meet [standard]" + tolerance data | Medium (depends on inspector) |
| "Is this factory legitimate?" | ISO + UL + CSA | Equipment manufacturer story + factory photos + visit invitation | Strong (more vivid than logos) |
| "Can I trust quality consistency?" | Cert implies QC | Batch traceability + 60% repeat rate + machine precision narrative | Strong |
| "What if there's a problem?" | Cert provides legal recourse | Free samples + factory-direct accountability + response commitment | Medium |

**Net assessment**: L2-L5 signals provide strong compensation for L1 gaps in most scenarios. The weakest point is regulatory compliance for markets requiring formal certification — here, the honest "in progress" positioning and cooperative application offer are the best available moves.
